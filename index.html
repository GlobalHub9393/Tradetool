      7FAQ / Trade Help

      Quick reference only. Always verify the current promotion, trade value, and eligibility in OPUS, CCKM, tradein.att.com, or the approved trade flow before quoting.

      1. How long until promo credits hit the account?

      Promo credits usually appear within 1–3 billing cycles. If credits do not show right away, the customer should keep paying the bill normally. Credits may catch up later if the promotion and trade were completed correctly.

      2. How long does the customer have to trade in the phone?

      Customers usually have 30 days to complete the trade-in. Best practice is to complete it same day, within 14 days, or at least within the same billing month whenever possible.

      3. What is the difference between trade value and promotion value?

      The trade value is what the trade system says the phone is worth. That value determines which promotion tier the customer qualifies for. A phone can have a lower actual trade value but still qualify for a larger bill-credit promotion.

      4. Do we quote the promo before checking trade value?

      No. Use the approved trade flow first. The model alone is not enough because value can change based on condition, activation lock, model accuracy, storage, and current trade rules.

      5. Who sends the trade-in phones?

      We send the phones through the approved trade-in process when completing the trade. Double-check the trade device, customer information, IMEI/model, and submission details before sending.

      6. Why does the tool say to verify in OPUS/CCKM?

      This tool is a sales guide and estimate. Promotions, eligible devices, plan requirements, and trade rules can change. OPUS, CCKM, and the approved trade flow are the final checks before quoting or completing the sale.

      7. How does the customer’s plan affect eligible promotions?

      The customer’s plan is one of the main indicators of which promotions they may be eligible for. Premium, Starter, Value, 55+, FirstNet, and CRU plan groups can qualify differently, so confirm the plan group before quoting.

      8. Can damaged or destroyed phones still qualify?

      Sometimes, yes. Damaged and sometimes even destroyed phones can still qualify depending on the current promotion and the value returned by the approved trade flow. Do not assume based on appearance alone — check the trade system before quoting.

      9. How does Next Up Anytime work?

      Next Up Anytime allows a customer to upgrade after paying one cycle with the new device. After 12 months or 33% paid, the customer can receive eligible promotions based on trade value, pays the remaining installments, and keeps the turned-in phone process tied to the offer. Before 12 months or 33% paid, AT&T pays the remaining installments and takes the phone. The device must be in perfect condition. Check with a manager and/or CCKM before quoting.

      10. What should reps double-check before submitting the order?

      Confirm the correct plan group, promo, device SKU/storage, trade value, trade deadline, Next Up status, customer expectations, and OPUS/CCKM eligibility before quoting or completing the sale.
